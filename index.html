Research Interests

Electron Paramagnetic Resonance (EPR)

The total energy of a system is a unique functional of the electron density. Probing the electronic spin density distribution by EPR therefore provides precise energetic and structural information for species harboring unpaired electrons. Questions concerning the structure-function relationship of molecular systems can often be addressed at ~0.1 A resolution.

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR)

While the project is still in the early stages, I am attempting to structurally characterize the interaction between the alpha and beta subunits of ribonucleotide reductase by NMR. By using a mixed isotopic labeling scheme I hope to detect and assign long-range 13C-15N dipolar contacts across the subunit interface with DNP MAS experiments.

Cryo-Electron Microscopy (Cryo-EM)

While my direct involvement in this project has been limited to negative stain sample preparation and 3D particle reconstruction, we are hopeful that a collaboration with the Drennan lab at MIT will allow for a successful cryo-EM 3D reconstruction of the ribonucleotide reductase a2b2 complex. This low resolution technique is complementary to higher resolution techniques such as EPR or NMR.
