Research Interests

Electron Paramagnetic Resonance (EPR)

The total energy of a system is a unique functional of the electron density. Probing the electronic spin density distribution by EPR therefore provides precise energetic and structural information for species harboring unpaired electrons. Questions concerning the structure-function relationship of molecular systems can often be addressed at ~0.1 A resolution.

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR)

While EPR is limited to systems with unpaired electron spins, NMR has the benefit of being applicable to all molecules of suitable size. With recent advances in sparse isotopic labeling techniques, higher magic angle spinning (MAS) speeds for proton detection, and sensitivity boosts from dynamic nuclear polarization, solid-state NMR is a powerful tool for protein structure determination.

Machine Learning

Machine learning allows computers to learn from a training dataset to make predicitons on datasets its never seen before. Supervised computer vision models, in particular, perform image recognition at levels beyond human performance for certain datasets. Can machine learning therefore be applied to automate or assist spectroscopic analysis? A growing body of literature (including my own) suggests the answer is yes.
